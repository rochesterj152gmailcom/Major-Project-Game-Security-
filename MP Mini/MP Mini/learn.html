Password and MFA

Stronger account protection starts with basic habits

One of the easiest ways to reduce account compromise is to stop reusing passwords and enable multi-factor authentication wherever possible. Even if a password is stolen, MFA can still help block attackers.

Use unique passwords

Each account should have its own password so one breach does not affect everything else.

Turn on MFA

Add a second layer of protection using an authenticator app, code, or approval prompt.

Protect your email too

Your email account is often the recovery point for your gaming accounts, so secure it first.

Safe Browsing

Think before you click

Safe browsing means slowing down and checking whether a site, file, or message looks legitimate before you interact with it.

🌐

Check URLs carefully

Look for spelling tricks, fake domains, and unusual endings before entering any login details.

⬇️

Avoid risky downloads

Only download games, updates, or tools from official stores and trusted sources.

🚨

Ignore fake urgency

Messages that pressure you to act immediately are often designed to make you panic and click.
